# Case Study: Wave Grocery Web Storefront
**Product Type:** Responsive E-commerce Web Application  
**Role:** Lead Product Designer  
**Client/Company:** Wave Grocery  

---

## Executive Summary
The Wave Grocery Storefront is a responsive web platform that allows grocery retailers to launch online storefronts quickly. As the Lead Product Designer, my mandate was to design an engaging, desktop-to-mobile shopping experience that replicates the familiarity of a physical store while providing high-performance e-commerce tools. I led the UX/UI design, theme customization framework, and key product discovery workflows.

---

## The Challenge: Merging Branding and Grocery Complexity
Like the mobile app, the web storefront needed to adapt to different retailers' branding using a single design system. Furthermore, grocery websites require complex UX solutions:
1.  **Massive Catalogs:** Supermarket websites list tens of thousands of SKUs, making clear information architecture and search vital.
2.  **Order Prep Complexity:** Fulfilling fresh food orders requires capturing customer preferences (e.g., meat cut thickness, banana ripeness) directly in the shopping cart.
3.  **Real-Time Stocking:** Product availability and prices fluctuate constantly based on physical store stock.

---

## My Role and Design Solutions

### 1. Web Implementation of the Multi-Brand Design System
I designed the web-based responsive components utilizing our token system.
*   **Theme Engine:** Created 30+ configuration design variables (logo alignment, grid vs. list card configurations, primary/secondary colors, typography pairings, banner ratios) that store managers can control from the admin panel. 
*   **Consistency:** Used the shared Figma library of 300+ components and 1000+ variants, ensuring design parity between the web storefront and the native mobile applications.

### 2. Crafting a Personalized Shopping Experience
I designed key personalization modules powered by our backend algorithms:
*   **Intelligent Search Experience:** Designed an advanced autocomplete and predictive search bar. As users type, it displays category shortcuts, brand suggestions, and instant product listings with pricing, significantly reducing search friction.
*   **Cutting & Preparation Preferences:** Standard e-commerce cart layouts do not support custom food preparation. I designed cart line-item extensions that let users specify cutting and preparation preferences (e.g., "grated parmesan," "thick-cut ribeye") for counter items.
*   **Behavioral Smart Lists:** Designed personal dashboards where users see "Frequently Bought Together" items, smart lists based on past grocery baskets, and a quick-reorder checkout sequence.

### 3. ERP & POS Synchronization Indicators
To handle inventory fluctuations, I designed real-time alert states:
*   **Stock Status Badges:** Dynamic, clean indicators for items that are low in stock, out of stock, or on promotion.
*   **Pre-checkout Substitute Selector:** Allowed customers to designate their substitute preferences (e.g., "Refund," "Call me," or "AI-suggested replacement") during checkout to avoid order delays.

---

## Impact & Business Outcomes
*   **Sales Growth:** Supported client expansion, including ANEDIK Kritikos Supermarkets, resulting in a **350% increase in online ordering volume**.
*   **Cart Completion:** Reduced cart abandonment rates by **25%** by designing cart-drawer quick actions and instant shopping list imports.
*   **Integration Scalability:** Ensured full design alignment across POS, ERP, and inventory integrations, enabling seamless updates of 50,000+ SKUs in real-time.
