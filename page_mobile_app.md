# Case Study: Wave Grocery Mobile Application
**Product Type:** Native iOS & Android White-Label Mobile Apps  
**Role:** Lead Product Designer  
**Client/Company:** Wave Grocery  

---

## Executive Summary
As the Lead Product Designer for Wave Grocery, I led the user experience and interface design for our native mobile applications. The mobile app enables traditional brick-and-mortar grocery retailers to transition into digital commerce, offering customers an on-the-go shopping experience that drives sales. My primary responsibility was to design a scalable, white-label mobile application that could be quickly deployed for different grocery brands while maintaining unique brand identity and delivering industry-leading mobile UX.

---

## The Challenge: Multi-Brand Scalability
The greatest design challenge was creating a single, unified codebase and design system that could serve multiple independent grocery retailers. Each retailer demanded a high level of differentiation so their mobile apps would not look identical to competitors using the same platform. 

We needed a system that went beyond simple logo swaps and color updates. We had to support diverse visual styles, layouts, feature toggles, and catalog structures—all while drawing from a single master design system.

---

## My Role and Design Strategy

### 1. Creating the Multi-Brand Design System
To solve this, I designed a vast, tokenized design system in Figma, establishing a robust variable hierarchy.
*   **Scale:** Over **300 core components** and **1,000+ variants** supporting iOS and Android design patterns.
*   **Variable Token Architecture:** Developed a three-tier design token structure (Global Tokens -> Semantic Tokens -> Component/Brand Override Tokens). This allowed us to swap brand themes (colors, typography, corner radii, shadows, border weights) in minutes.
*   **Visual Differentiation:** Retails could choose between distinct aesthetic directions (e.g., modern minimalist rounded borders vs. classic sharp-edged serif styles) built directly into the system parameters, ensuring no two deployed apps felt similar.

### 2. Designing Supermarket-Specific UX Flows
Supermarket shopping is highly complex compared to standard e-commerce. I designed custom mobile patterns to handle:
*   **Weighted Products Flow:** Fruits, vegetables, and deli meats are sold by weight. I designed a dual-selection control that allows customers to toggle between ordering by quantity (e.g., 3 apples) and by weight (e.g., 500 grams), paired with a "picker preference note" text area (e.g., "ripe bananas," "cheese sliced extra thin").
*   **Smart Lists & Cart Building:** Supermarket baskets average 40+ items. I designed "smart list" templates and repeat-order widgets that let customers add their weekly essentials to their cart in under a minute.
*   **Dietary and Nutritional Filtering:** Created a comprehensive filtering system where users can save dietary profiles (e.g., gluten-free, vegan, nut allergy) that automatically flag or hide non-compliant products in search results.

### 3. Native Performance & Micro-Animations
I worked closely with mobile engineers to optimize native performance and micro-interactions. I created prototypes for cart-addition feedback, quick-swipe list removals, and animated category transitions, which significantly increased the tactile feedback and delight of the app.

---

## Impact & Business Outcomes
*   **App Store Performance:** Attained a **4.7-star average rating** across Google Play and Apple App Store.
*   **Conversion Increase:** Drove a **130% higher conversion rate** compared to mobile web browser ordering.
*   **Time-to-Market:** Reduced client onboarding and mobile deployment design cycles by **70%** through the automated multi-brand design system.
