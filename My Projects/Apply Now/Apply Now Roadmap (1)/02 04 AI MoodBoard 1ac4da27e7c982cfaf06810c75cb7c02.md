### The Process & Benefits of Rapid Mapping with Google Stitch

Using Google Stitch to rapidly map different layout solutions is an incredibly effective UX strategy. When transitioning from abstract user flows to actual interfaces, it is very common to have multiple competing ideas in your head about how the screen should be organized. By using a tool designed for rapid "stitching" and drafting, you bypass the trap of getting bogged down in high-fidelity details—like exact hex codes or border radii—too early in the process.

The primary benefit of this approach is **velocity**. It allows you to quickly get the ideas out of your head and onto the screen, giving you a much better picture of how the final platform is going to look and feel. Because the investment of time per layout is so low, you have the psychological freedom to experiment with bold or unconventional layouts. You can easily compare two distinct versions of the Candidate Info page side-by-side, evaluate them against your user personas, and quickly discard the ones that don't work, ensuring that your final high-fidelity design is built on a validated and structurally sound foundation.
