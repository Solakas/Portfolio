### Persona 1: Sarah, The High-Volume Recruiter

**Background Info:** Sarah works in Talent Acquisition for a large retail and customer success company. She manages dozens of active job postings simultaneously, often dealing with hundreds of inbound applicants per role. She is constantly fighting against the clock.

**Goals:**

- Achieve maximum speed and efficiency in processing candidate pipelines.
- Filter out unqualified applicants instantly to focus only on viable candidates.
- Reduce the time spent performing repetitive administrative tasks.

**Pain Points:**

- **Click Fatigue:** Extremely frustrated by systems that force her to click multiple times or open new tabs just to see basic contact info or read a resume.
- **Hidden Actions:** Hates when bulk actions (like mass-rejecting candidates) are buried in dropdown menus instead of being immediately accessible.

**Usage & Behaviors:**

- Lives primarily in the **Candidates List** view.
- Relies heavily on persistent "dealbreaker" filters (e.g., Right-to-Work toggles, Location radius sliders).
- Scans candidate rows using the "6-second rule," looking only at Current Title, Location, and Application Date before making a decision.

<aside>
🔑

**Key Point (Most Important Feature)**: 
Frictionless Bulk Processing & Scannability.  Sarah’s success depends entirely on the Candidates List view acting as a highly scannable, instantly filterable spreadsheet where bulk actions (Reject/Advance) appear the moment she selects multiple checkboxes.

</aside>

### Persona 2: David, The Technical Recruiter

**Background Info:** David is a Senior IT Recruiter hiring specialized developers and data scientists. His application volume is lower than Sarah's, but the required candidate quality and specific skill matching are exponentially higher. He acts as the bridge between candidates and highly technical engineering managers.

**Goals:**

- Gain deep, accurate context on a candidate's actual skills (hard vs. soft skills).
- Collaborate seamlessly with hiring managers without leaving the platform.
- Evaluate candidates holistically beyond just the keywords on their resume.

**Pain Points:**

- **Rigid Parsing:** Frustrated by traditional ATS parsers that incorrectly format or reject highly qualified developers based on strict keyword rules.
- **Siloed Communication:** Hates relying on external email chains or Slack to share profiles and gather feedback from hiring managers.
- **Context Switching:** Struggles with platforms that don't allow him to look at a candidate's portfolio/resume while simultaneously typing out a scorecard.

**Usage & Behaviors:**

- Spends most of his time on the **Candidates Info Page**.
- Uses complex keyword searches, boolean logic, and custom tags (e.g., "React AND Node.js") to filter his candidate list.
- Frequently uses quick actions like `@tagging` colleagues in the notes section and utilizing "Quick Rate" (star ratings) after initial screenings.

<aside>
🔑

**Key Point** : 
The Unified Evaluation Workspace (Split-Screen UI).
David absolutely requires a dual-pane interface on the Info Page. He needs a native, inline PDF viewer for the resume on one side, and his interactive workspace (scorecards, team notes, portfolio links) on the other, eliminating the need to download files or switch tabs.

</aside>

### Persona 3: Elena, The HR Director

**Background Info:** Elena oversees the entire enterprise hiring strategy. She manages a team of recruiters (including Sarah and David) and is responsible for compliance, high-level reporting, and long-term talent pooling. She focuses on the macro-level health of the hiring pipeline.

**Goals:**

- Maintain impeccable data integrity across all active and past campaigns.
- Build and leverage a reliable "Talent Vault" to re-engage past applicants when new roles open up, saving money on external job ads.
- Ensure all hiring practices are documented, compliant, and fair.

**Pain Points:**

- **"Garbage In, Garbage Out":** Her biggest frustration is when fast-moving recruiters fail to leave standardized notes or rejection reasons, making the database messy and unsearchable later.
- **Lost Historical Context:** Hates when a candidate applies for a new role and the system fails to alert the team that this person was already interviewed last year.

**Usage & Behaviors:**

- Utilizes the platform for cross-campaign searches and administrative reporting rather than day-to-day active screening.
- Relies on historical filters (e.g., Past Application Status, Application Date range picker) to search the global database.
- Frequently performs actions like assigning past "Silver Medalist" candidates to new job requisitions or exporting pipeline data to CSV.

<aside>
🔑

**Key Point** : 
The Historical Talent Vault & Badging.
Elena's primary need is data continuity. The platform must clearly display visual indicators (badges) on the Candidates List if an applicant has a history with the company, and the Info Page must have a dedicated "History" tab detailing their past pipeline stages and aggregate scorecards.

</aside>
